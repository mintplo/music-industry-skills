# Market Trend

## Use this branch

Use for a genre, platform, country, audience, or market-pattern question that
requires a bounded sample rather than a claim about a single artist.

## Questions to resolve

Define the population, sample, geography, and observation window before
collecting examples. Specify what pattern would support the inquiry and what
would count as a counterexample.

## Source stack

Use [Web search](../providers/web-search.md) to discover official artist,
label, platform, chart, and attributable reporting across the defined sample.
Use [Web crawling](../providers/web-crawling.md) only to review an allowed
direct page. Add [Circle Chart](../providers/circle-chart.md),
[Oricon](../providers/oricon.md), or [YouTube](../providers/youtube.md) only
when their specific market or public signals answer the question. Use
[MusicBrainz](../providers/musicbrainz.md) or
[Wikidata](../providers/wikidata.md) when entity identity or release context
needs resolution. When the sample depends on whether a song belongs to a release
or precedes it, use one catalog or entity provider to verify that relation in
addition to web discovery.

## Evidence checks

State a candidate pattern that goes beyond the sample's selection rule, then
weigh it against supporting examples and meaningful exceptions or
counterexamples. Explain whether those exceptions weaken, narrow, or refute the
pattern. If the bounded search cannot test the pattern well enough, say so.

Label inferences as inferences, preserve source dates and markets, and state
sampling limits, missing evidence, and access constraints. Do not generalize
from one artist, present a bounded sample as a universal rule, or infer causal
impact from descriptive cases.

## Completion criterion

Close the requested pattern with a bounded, sourced inference that reflects the
supporting evidence, meaningful exceptions, and sampling limits. Use only the
output shape that helps answer the request, and make uncertainty visible when
the available sample is insufficient, unavailable, or access-dependent.
