# Web Crawling

## Capabilities

Extract permitted static-page content and, only when necessary, render a page
through an available browser or agent-browser tool for direct-source review.

## Use when

A direct official page is needed after [Web search](web-search.md) discovery and
normal static HTML does not expose the relevant content.

## Do not use for

Permission to bypass login, paywalls, Cloudflare or other anti-bot controls,
robots directives, rate limits, or site terms.

## Access

Try ordinary static HTML first. Use an active browser or agent-browser second
only when necessary and authorized by the page's normal access path. Apply the
[Robots Exclusion Protocol](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc9309) alongside
the target site's terms; robots rules are not access authorization.

## Inputs and outputs

Start with a direct URL and the precise claim to verify. Retain the final URL,
publisher, title, publication or update date, quoted or paraphrased supporting
content, access time, and whether static or rendered access was used.

## Evidence

Authority tier: depends on the target page. A direct official page can be
primary evidence; a third-party page remains third-party evidence even when
rendered successfully. Cite the target page, not the crawling method.

## Limits and terms

Stop at access controls, explicit disallowance, or terms that prohibit the
intended use. Rendered content can change by locale, session, and time; do not
reconstruct deleted pages or claim complete site coverage.

## Fallbacks

Return to [Web search](web-search.md) for snippets plus direct official pages,
use an official press release or archived first-party page when permitted, or
close the claim as unavailable or access-dependent.

## Verification

Open the direct source in the allowed path, verify that it supports the claim,
and preserve its date, market, and access conditions. Record why crawling was
needed and never elevate a snippet into final evidence.
